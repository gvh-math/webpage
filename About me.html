My inspiring professors

I spent two beautiful years at the Universite Paris-Saclay, there I have discovered amazing techniques (and the passion) to study fluid mechanics PDEs, in particular the Navier-Stokes equations, from the hand of two expert in the field: Pierre-Gilles Lemarie-Rieusset and Diego Chamorro.

In 2020, I visited for six months the Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg, in Germany. There, I knew the commitment, discipline and lucid of professor Enrique Zuazua, whose advice, support and hospitality have made me enjoy even more doing research in mathematics.

In my PhD in Bordeaux, France, I have had the opportunity to get to know the wonderful world of mathematical research, where the advice, ideas and support of professors Franck Sueur and Marius Tucsnak have been fundamental in my development as a researcher.

When I got started to learning maths during my bachelor at the University of Valparaíso, Chile, I knew the passion and hard work for learning mathematics in my experiences with professors Jesús Juyumaya, Eduardo Stange. At the final step of this first adventure, I worked with Amalia Pizarro-Madariaga, who was my advisor in my final work in that step. After finishing this perior, I moved for doing some teaching and more studying my master in Temuco (a beautiful land in the south of Chile), I had the opportunity to learn about group theory, and about the rigorousness of the mathematical work with Professor Angel Carocca, from the University of the Frontier.
